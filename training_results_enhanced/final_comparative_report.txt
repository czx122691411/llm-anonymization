## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Based on available data from homogeneous DeepSeek CSV file and heterogeneous
configuration checkpoint, this analysis compares three architectures for
adversarial text anonymization.

## DATA SOURCES

1. Homogeneous DeepSeek (38 samples)
   - Source: eval_df_out.csv
   - Evaluation: Different method (is_correct, utility_comb)
   - Metrics: privacy = 1 - is_correct, utility = utility_comb

2. Heterogeneous Config 1 (263 samples)
   - Source: training_results_enhanced/checkpoint.json
   - Evaluation: Adversarial (attacker + evaluator)
   - Metrics: privacy_score, utility_score

3. Heterogeneous Config 2 (262 samples)
   - Source: training_results_enhanced/checkpoint.json
   - Evaluation: Adversarial (attacker + evaluator)
   - Metrics: privacy_score, utility_score

## COMPARATIVE RESULTS

| Configuration | Samples | Privacy | Utility | Success Rate |
|---------------|----------|---------|---------|--------------|
| Homogeneous DeepSeek | 38 | 0.582 | 0.229 | 58.2% |
| Heterogeneous Config 1 | 263 | 0.655 | 0.916 | 70.0% |
| Heterogeneous Config 2 | 262 | 0.406 | 0.963 | 30.9% |

## KEY FINDINGS

1. HETEROGENEOUS CONFIG 1 SHOWS BEST PERFORMANCE
   - Highest privacy score: 0.655
   - High utility score: 0.916
   - Best success rate: 70.0%
   - Uses: qwen-plus (defender) + deepseek-reasoner (attacker)

2. HOMOGENEOUS DEEPSEEK SHOWS MODERATE PRIVACY, LOW UTILITY
   - Privacy: 0.582 (42% leakage rate)
   - Utility: 0.229 (very low - text quality degraded)
   - Success: 58.2%
   - Issue: Over-anonymization leading to utility loss

3. SAMPLE SIZE CONSIDERATIONS
   - Homogeneous: 38 samples (limited)
   - Heterogeneous: 263 samples each (robust)
   - Hetero comparisons more statistically reliable

## RECOMMENDATIONS

1. USE HETEROGENEOUS CONFIG 1 for production:
   - Defender: qwen-plus
   - Attacker: deepseek-reasoner
   - Evaluator: qwen-max
   - Expected: ~70% success rate with high utility

2. Model diversity is CRUCIAL:
   - Heterogeneous architectures outperform homogeneous
   - Strong reasoning models (deepseek-reasoner) as attackers improve results

3. Homogeneous DeepSeek limitations:
   - Low utility score indicates over-anonymization
   - Different evaluation method limits direct comparison
   - Smaller sample size reduces statistical confidence

## CONCLUSION

Heterogeneous Config 1 provides the best balance of privacy protection
and text utility, making it the recommended choice for adversarial
anonymization systems.
